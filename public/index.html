Seb Dalgarno

PhD Student and Human in the World

University of Northern British Columbia

I am a PhD student at the UNBC Freshwater Fish Ecology lab. We are using many years of acoustic telemetry data and Bayesian models to make inferences about White Sturgeon movement in the Columbia River.

I was previously a Computational Biologist at Poisson Consulting, currently owner of North Beach Consulting and am very enthusiastic about R, spatial data/maps and (interactive) data visualization.

I am also a songwriter and surfer living on the north coast of Haida Gwaii. You can check out recordings from my band Rusty Mould and the Sugar Agates.

Projects

I enjoy developing open-source R packages, Shiny apps, and web-based interactive data visualizations (including web maps). Here are some examples:

All Web Map R Package Shiny App Other

Shinyrems

A Shiny application and R package for downloading, cleaning, visualizing and analyzing data from the BC Environmental Monitoring System (EMS) database.

Peachland-Trepanier Logging History

A web map and interactive bar chart of the logging history of Peachland and Trepanier watersheds in British columbia.

Fwapgr

An R client for the fwapg pg-featureserv web API used to get data from the BC Freshwater Atlas.
